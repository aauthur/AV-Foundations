# Limits (epsilon–delta)

Welcome to lesson 1.

## Definition
We say $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$ if for every $\varepsilon > 0$ there exists $\delta > 0$
such that $0 < |x-a| < \delta$ implies $|f(x)-L| < \varepsilon$.

## Example
Prove $\lim_{x \to 2} (3x+1)=7$.

**Proof idea:** pick $\delta=\varepsilon/3$.
