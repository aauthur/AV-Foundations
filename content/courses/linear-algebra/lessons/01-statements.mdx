# Statements and Systems of Equations

In this series on linear algebra, we want to offer several different perspectives to make it easier for the student to get a complete picture of what's going on. Most popularly, geometry is used 
to give students intuition, but although this is excellent for visualizing mathematical ideas, linear algebra frequently deals with higher dimensions where our visual notions of geometry collapse. We
want to think of equations as *statements*. Later on we will also introduce the *function* perspective.

## Definition
A declarative statement in mathematics is a statement that is either true or false.
